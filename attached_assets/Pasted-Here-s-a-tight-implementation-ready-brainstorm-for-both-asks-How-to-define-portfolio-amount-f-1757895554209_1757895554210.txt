# How to define “portfolio amount” for a firm that manages players

Think of it like AUM for athletes. Don’t use just Total Contract Value (TCV); build a layered stack so it’s accurate, risk-aware, and revenue-relevant.

## Core portfolio value stack (choose 1–3 as your primary metric; show the rest as lenses)

1. **TCV-UM (Total Contract Value Under Management)**
   Sum of all active contract face values for represented athletes (avoid double counting multi-team obligations).

   * Pros: simple, comparable.
   * Cons: ignores guarantees, options, risk.

2. **GCV-UM (Guaranteed Contract Value Under Management)**
   Sum of *guaranteed* portions only.

3. **NPV-UM (Discounted Cash Flow of Contracts)**

   $$
   \text{NPV-UM}=\sum_i \sum_t \frac{\text{ExpectedCashflow}_{i,t}}{(1+r)^t}
   $$

   * Use team/league-appropriate discount rate $r$; expected cashflow includes incentives × probability.

4. **EV-UM (Probability-Weighted Contract EV)**

   $$
   \text{EV-UM}=\sum_i (\text{Guaranteed}_i + \text{NonGuaranteed}_i \cdot p_i + \text{Incentives}_i \cdot q_i)
   $$

   * $p_i$=probability of vesting/non-guarantee converting; $q_i$=incentive hit probability.

5. **BRV-UM (Brand & Endorsement Revenue Under Management)**
   Annualized endorsement value + appearance fees + rev-share media/social monetization (probability-weighted and discounted).

6. **CLV-UM (Career Lifetime Value Under Management)**
   Projection of next N years (contract + brand), discounted, with age/position curves and attrition.

7. **RARV-UM (Risk-Adjusted Portfolio Value)**

   $$
   \text{RARV-UM}=\sum_i \text{NPV}_i \cdot (1 - \text{RiskHaircut}_i)
   $$

   * Haircut from injury risk, suspension/PR risk, performance volatility, roster uncertainty, cap constraints.

8. **FRV-UM (Firm Revenue Value)** — what *you* actually monetize

   $$
   \text{FRV-UM}=\sum_i \left(\text{MgmtFee\%}_i \cdot \text{ContractCashflow}_i + \text{BrandFee\%}_i \cdot \text{EndorsementCashflow}_i\right)\ \text{(NPV/EV basis)}
   $$

   * This is the best “business KPI”.

## Add supporting portfolio diagnostics

* **Concentration**: HHI by athlete, team, league, brand category.
* **Duration ladder**: value maturing by year (helps renewal pipeline planning).
* **Liquidity**: next-12-months cashflows vs long-dated.
* **Sensitivity**: ΔPortfolio per 1% change in Gravity Score / engagement / win share (DV01-style).
* **VaR & Stress**: 95% one-year downside given injury/performance shocks.
* **Pipeline EV**: expected value of negotiations × close probability.
* **Quality flags**: % guaranteed vs non-guaranteed; options mix (player/team/TO/PO); incentive dependency.

## Data hygiene & anti-double-counting rules

* Split base vs incentives; attach explicit probabilities.
* Mark overlapping deal periods; don’t sum conflicting obligations.
* Separate *signed* vs *MOU/rumor*; pipeline lives outside AUM.
* Normalize currencies; record discount/FX assumptions.

## Quick metric recipes (to hand your devs/analysts)

* **Contract EV per athlete**

  $$
  \text{EV} = \text{Guaranteed} + \sum_k \text{NonG}_k \cdot p_k + \sum_m \text{Incentive}_m \cdot q_m
  $$

* **FRV (firm-take)**

  $$
  \text{FRV}=\sum_i \left( \alpha_i \cdot \text{ContractCashflow}_i + \beta_i \cdot \text{BrandCashflow}_i \right) \ \text{(NPV)}
  $$

  where $\alpha_i, \beta_i$ are fee percentages by client/deal.

* **Risk haircut**

  $$
  \text{Haircut}_i = 1 - \prod_f (1-h_{i,f})
  $$

  (factors $f$: injury, PR, performance, roster; calibrate $h$ from history)

* **Portfolio concentration (HHI)**

  $$
  \text{HHI}=\sum_i s_i^2,\quad s_i=\frac{\text{EV}_i}{\sum_j \text{EV}_j}
  $$

* **Sensitivity (DV01-style)**

  $$
  \Delta \text{Portfolio} \approx \frac{\partial \text{EV}}{\partial G} \cdot \Delta G
  $$

  where $G$=Gravity Score / engagement index.
