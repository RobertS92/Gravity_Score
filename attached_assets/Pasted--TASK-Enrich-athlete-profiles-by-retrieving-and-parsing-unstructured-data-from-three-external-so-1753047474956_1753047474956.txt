### TASK: Enrich athlete profiles by retrieving and parsing unstructured data from three external sources: Google News, Social Bios, and Wikipedia.

---

### INPUT:
A dictionary for each athlete containing:
- name
- twitter_handle
- instagram_handle
- wikipedia_url

---

### OUTPUT:
Augmented fields to be added to the dataset per athlete:

#### 📘 From Wikipedia:
- `wiki_awards_mentions`: List of awards, honors, recognitions detected in the page
- `wiki_injury_mentions`: Any injury-related entries (section or sentence)
- `wiki_charity_mentions`: Keywords related to philanthropy, nonprofits, or foundations
- `wiki_controversy_mentions`: Any public legal/PR issues found ("arrest", "suspension", etc.)
- `wiki_positive_sentiment_score`: VADER/TextBlob sentiment of key sections
- `wiki_negative_sentiment_score`: Same, for negative polarity

#### 📰 From Google News:
- `news_mentions_30d`: Number of news articles mentioning the player in last 30 days
- `news_positive_articles_count`: Number of positively classified articles
- `news_negative_articles_count`: Number of negatively classified articles
- `news_latest_titles`: Top 5 headlines with links and timestamps
- `buzz_spike_score`: % increase vs previous 30-day baseline (if history available)
- `controversial_keywords`: ["suspended", "fined", "released", "arrested"] occurrences

#### 📲 From Social Media Bios (Twitter, Instagram):
- `bio_keywords`: Extracted relevant keywords (e.g., "Entrepreneur", "Vegan", "Nike Athlete")
- `bio_sponsor_alignment`: Cosine similarity between bio and known brand descriptions (if embeddings available)
- `bio_sentiment`: Polarity score of bio text (range -1 to 1)
- `bio_named_entities`: Brands, orgs, or affiliations mentioned (NER extraction)

---

### LLM Subtasks:

1. **Wikipedia**
   - Fetch Wikipedia content from URL
   - Extract:
     - Award mentions → keywords like "Pro Bowl", "All-Pro", "MVP"
     - Injury history → look for “torn”, “out for”, “missed season”, etc.
     - Philanthropy → keywords like “charity”, “founded”, “donated”
     - Controversy → “arrested”, “fined”, “banned”, “controversy”
   - Run sentiment analysis per section

2. **Google News**
   - Use player name to query Google News
   - Collect top 20 headlines from the past 30 days
   - Classify each headline:
     - Is it positive, neutral, or negative?
     - Does it mention buzzwords like “deal”, “endorsement”, “injury”, “arrest”?
   - Count, summarize, and calculate buzz/spike score if time series available

3. **Social Media Bios**
   - Scrape or extract bios from handles (or use stored bios)
   - Perform:
     - Sentiment analysis
     - NER (detect brand affiliations, sponsors, causes)
     - Cosine similarity between bio and a corpus of brand positioning statements
     - Pull keywords and hashtags for alignment modeling

---

### NOTE:
Use schema-aligned field names for output. Normalize scores to 0–1 where applicable. Store original text (bios, headlines, wiki sections) as optional fields for traceability.
